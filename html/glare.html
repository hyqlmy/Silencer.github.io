单词：glare     [ɡlɛə]

翻译：n. 强光；怒视；炫耀     释义：a light within the field of vision that is brighter than the brightness to which the eyes are adapted;
近义词：
  • flame
  • glitter
  • flame
  • gleam
反义词：
    形近词：
    • large
    • flare
    例句:
    1. I like turning a corner from the shade of a house into the full torrid glare of an open space.

      我喜欢将来自房子的色度的一个角落变成开着空间的完整晒焦的闪耀光。

    2. These sunglasses are designed to reduce glare.

      这些太阳镜是为减少刺眼的强光而设计的。

    3. The glare of the oncoming headlights temporarily blinded me.

      迎面车灯刺眼的光照得我瞬间什么也看不见。

    4. Eyelashes defend the eye by shading it from glare.

      眼睫毛通过遮掩眼睛而使眼睛避免受眩光刺激。

    5. Number denoting the degree to which discomfort glare is controlled.

      代表对不舒适眩光进行控制的等级号码。

    6. I started to offer help, but the fierce glare on his face stopped me.

      我开始提出要帮助他，但是他那种凶狠瞪视的目光使我却步。
